My Story

Musical Training

  • Regular attendee at Australian Youth Orchestra camps as a teenager

  • Accepted into the Prelude Program, allowing study at university level whilst completing year 12

  • Awarded a full scholarship at the University of Tasmania

  • Placed on the Dean's Role of Excellence for all three full-time years at university

After completing university, I spent time travelling and living in the UK, exploring the Irish and Shetland fiddle traditions and playing for enjoyment. On returning to Australia, I focussed on building my teaching studio in Melbourne and encouraging students of all ages and abilities to develop sound violin technique and a love of music.



Move to IT

Finding myself with extra time during the pandemic, I began filming video lessons to help my students practice more effectively at home. I started out filming simple lessons and making small edits using iMovie, but quickly realised I needed more skills to produce the content that I had in mind. I finally found Da Vinci Resolve, which allowed me to produce more informative and engaging videos (I still can't believe this awesome software is totally free!).

Over time, this project turned into a short course in violin playing which I uploaded to teachable. The more skills I developed, the more curious I became to find out how technology worked and how I could produce higher quality content. Whilst studying my Graduate Certificate in Cyber Security, I enjoyed understanding the programming languages driving websites and applications that we interact with everyday. After completing this course, I began studying for my CompTIA A+ certification which began filling in further gaps in my knowledge regarding how computers work at a hardware level and interact with the programming languages I had been studying.


In my spare time I enjoy performing and experimenting with pedals and sound-design software on my electric violin, taking and editing nature photos (including the headers on this page), spending time with family and friends and keeping fit.
